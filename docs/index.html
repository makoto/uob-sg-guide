Singapore District Liveability Study Data Guide

Geospatial analysis of perceived liveability across five Singapore planning districts: Queenstown, Bishan, Outram, Tampines, and Newton.

Disclaimer: This content is AI-generated. The author does not guarantee the accuracy of the analysis. Users should treat this as a guide and always refer to the original data sources to verify.

3D Buildings Viewer

Multi-district viewer with district selector (Queenstown, Bishan, Outram, Tampines, Newton). Extruded 3D buildings for all 5 districts (Queenstown: 8,671; Bishan: 3,766; Outram: 1,589; Tampines: 2,910; Newton: 759) with height-based colour coding, 27 toggleable overlays in 10 collapsible groups with feature counts per layer, provenance filter (Gov.sg / Global), and a categorized choropleth accordion with 30 subzone-level metrics across 5 sub-categories (with type badges: CNT/RATE/IDX/SAT). All gov-sg layers available for all districts. Street network, remote sensing (subzone-level + RS grid + raster PNGs), and walkability available for all 5 districts. Built with CesiumJS and 3D Tiles.

District selector: Switch between Queenstown, Bishan, Outram, Tampines, and Newton. Each district loads its own boundary, subzones, gov-sg layers, 3D building tileset, and choropleth data. Buildings, street network, remote sensing (subzone-level + RS grid + raster PNGs), and walkability (scores + grids) are available for all 5 districts. Missing data is handled gracefully.

HDB blocks layer: 308 HDB dots coloured by construction era — red (pre-1980), orange (1980–1999), green (2000+). Click shows year completed, dwelling units, and use flags.

Height control zones: Semi-transparent URA polygons showing storey limits from the Master Plan.

Street network layer: Road edges from OSMnx drive network, coloured by betweenness centrality to highlight key corridors, with dynamic legend. Available for all 5 districts (Queenstown: 3,788; Bishan: 1,363; Outram: 711; Tampines: 4,144; Newton: 838 edges).

Intersection density grid: 100m grid cells coloured by intersection density, computed via spatial kernel method (counts walk-network nodes with degree≥3 within a 300m radius, divided by circle area). Available for all 5 districts (Queenstown: 2,178; Bishan: 769; Outram: 135; Tampines: 2,085; Newton: 210 cells). Click a cell to see the intersection density value and method description.

Remote sensing layers: Subzone-level remote sensing data available for all 5 districts (used by choropleth). Pixel-level raster overlays (NDVI, LST, NDBI, canopy cover) and 100m RS grid now available for all 5 districts (Queenstown: 2,394 cells; Bishan: 840; Outram: 175; Tampines: 2,230; Newton: 265).

Choropleth heatmap: A collapsible accordion in the Layers panel organizes 30 metrics into 5 sub-categories (Demographics & Housing, Amenities & Infrastructure, Buildings, Remote Sensing, Walkability). Each metric has a coloured type badge (CNT/RATE/IDX/SAT). Click a metric to colour subzones; use the “Off” button to deactivate. Metrics include population density, elderly share, amenity density, MRT stations, cycling paths, park connectors, resale flat price, avg building height, HDB blocks, total buildings, max building height, resale transactions, avg HDB year built, dwelling units, dwelling density, plus 5 green/blue infrastructure metrics: green space coverage (%), green space area (km²), ABC Waters area (km²), NParks tracks (km), and sport facilities, plus 6 remote sensing indicators: Vegetation (NDVI), Built-up (NDBI), Land Surface Temp, Tree canopy cover, GHSL building height, and Surface elevation, plus 4 walkability metrics: Intersection density, Transit access, Destination access, and Walkability (BEH-NWI). Uses a 5-step YlOrRd colour ramp with dynamic legend.
